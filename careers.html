Medical Officer

A Medical Officer is a qualified physician who provides medical care to military personnel. They are responsible for diagnosing and treating illnesses and injuries, conducting health assessments, and ensuring the overall health and readiness of troops.

Apply Now

Intelligence Officer

An Intelligence Officer gathers, analyzes, and interprets information to support military operations. They are responsible for collecting data on enemy capabilities, intentions, and movements, as well as providing assessments that aid in strategic planning and decision-making.

Apply Now

Tradok (Training Officer)

A Tradok, or Training Officer, is responsible for the planning, coordination, and execution of training programs for military personnel. Their role involves assessing training needs, developing training curricula, and ensuring that service members acquire the necessary skills and knowledge to perform their duties effectively.

Apply Now

Cadet Police

A C.P., or Cadet Police Officer, typically refers to an individual responsible for ensuring the safety and security of civilians in a military context. This role may involve coordinating security operations, implementing safety protocols, and working with local law enforcement to protect communities during military operations or in conflict zones.

Apply Now

Special Force

Special Forces refers to highly trained military units that are skilled in unconventional warfare, counter-terrorism, and special reconnaissance missions. Members of Special Forces undergo rigorous training and are often deployed in situations that require specialized skills, such as hostage rescue, intelligence gathering, and direct action against high-value targets.

Apply Now
